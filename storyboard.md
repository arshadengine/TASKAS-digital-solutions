# TASKAS — Phase 2: Storyboard

## Site-Wide Cinematic Grammar

**Shell logic:** Full-viewport dark slabs. Every section occupies meaningful vertical space (min 60vh for hero, min 40vh for content sections). No cramped card collections.

**Navigation posture:** Minimal floating bar — translucent, amber-underlined active indicator, no hamburger, no mega-menu. Logo left (wordmark), 4 anchor links right. Disappears on scroll-down, reappears on scroll-up.

**Framing rules:** Content is never edge-to-edge. Left-biased or center-void compositions. Typographic anchors are large enough to be structural, not decorative.

**Density cadence:** Spectacle → Sparse → Dense → Breathing → Spectacle. Never two dense sections adjacent.

**Recurring atmospheric layers:** Fog grain texture (3% opacity SVG noise), amber sub-horizon glow (radial gradient bottom-center), ice edge-light (linear gradient from right edge).

**Composition family:** Cutaway monolith — content panels that open like apertures in a vast architectural surface.

---

## Page Narrative Arc

TASKAS site arc (Villeneuve's "dread-to-revelation" structure adapted to agency context):

1. **VOID** — You enter the system. Massive scale, silence, a single name. (Hero)
2. **SIGNAL** — Something moves. A brief establishing statement about who this entity is. (About)
3. **ARCHITECTURE** — The structure reveals itself. What this system can do. (Services)
4. **EVIDENCE** — Numbers. Cold, precise, undeniable. (Stats / Why Us)
5. **CONTACT** — The aperture opens. An invitation into the system. (CTA / Contact)
6. **END TITLE** — Footer. Like end credits rolling on a black field. (Footer)

## Scene Breakdown

### Scene 1 — HERO: "The Monolith"

**Page-level visual thesis:** A single architectural wordmark appears in atmospheric fog. Nothing else exists until it does.

**Hero dominance statement:** The logo/name TASKAS occupies architectural scale — 12vw minimum — suspended in amber-edged void, as if projected onto a building-sized LED surface in fog.

**Signature composition:** Diagonal split-atmosphere — left hemisphere is deep charcoal void, right hemisphere carries the amber fog glow, and the wordmark crosses both, lit from inside. The tagline below in status-label style. No hero image, no device mockup. Typography IS the architecture.

**Restraint statement:** No hero illustration, no particle rain, no animated gradient blob. The atmosphere moves, the text does not.

**Hero elements (3 minimum):** (1) Architectural TASKAS wordmark at colossal scale, (2) Atmospheric fog layer with amber right-glow, (3) Floating status label beneath tagline — `[ DIGITAL SOLUTIONS AGENCY ]` — in caps/mist style.

**Interaction:** Subtle parallax on mouse — fog layer drifts 12px opposite to cursor. One heavy interaction budget spent here.

---

### Scene 2 — ABOUT: "The Signal"

**Scene thesis:** A horizontal aperture cut revealing a brief statement of identity. Like a transmission breaking through static.

**Signature composition:** One large pull-quote line stacked on the left, with a thin amber vertical rule at left edge. Right side holds 2-column dense body text. Asymmetric — 55/45 split. The amber rule is the only color element in this section.

**Restraint statement:** No team photos, no about-image, no biographic card layout.

**Visual elements:** Amber left-border rule (3px, 60% section height), mist-colored body text, label above in caps.

---

### Scene 3 — SERVICES: "The Architecture"

**Scene thesis:** Six chambers in the monolith open one by one — each service is a contained vault door revealing its interior.

**Signature composition:** Vertical stacked service rows — each row is full-width, edge-to-edge, with service number in Orbitron at 4rem (left), service name at 2.5rem (center-left), and a brief 2-line descriptor at right. On hover, the row expands height and reveals a sub-line in amber. NOT a card grid.

**Restraint statement:** No service icons, no card shadows, no gradient-filled boxes.

**Visual elements:** Horizontal hairline separators (1px, dust color), amber expand-indicator on hover (right edge arrow), service number in mist color transforms to amber on hover.

---

### Scene 4 — WHY US / STATS: "The Evidence"

**Scene thesis:** Cold numerical authority. Like a data readout in a control room — numbers that validate the system's scale.

**Signature composition:** 4 large stat blocks in a 2×2 layout, each stat occupying its own monolith quadrant. Numbers in Orbitron at 5rem, counter-animate on scroll-enter. Label below in all-caps mist. Section background switches to `--void-alt` for contrast. Left side carries a vertical text treatment — "TRUSTED BY GROWING BUSINESSES" rotated 90°.

**Restraint statement:** No testimonial cards, no client logos grid, no star ratings.

**Visual elements:** Thin grid hairlines separating quadrants (dust color), amber glow behind each number at 6% opacity.

---

### Scene 5 — CONTACT CTA: "The Aperture"

**Scene thesis:** The monolith opens fully. A single commanding invitation — no form clutter, just the call.

**Signature composition:** Full-width void section. Center-aligned. One massive CTA headline at 5rem+. Below it, a single-line email/link in amber. Then one bordered button — no fill, amber 1px border, text `INITIATE PROJECT`. Entire section has the deepest black background of the site.

**Restraint statement:** No contact form in this section, no address block, no social links spam.

**Visual elements:** Ambient ice glow from top-center (frost radial gradient), amber bottom-edge line, letter-spacing on button text at 0.4em.

---

### Scene 6 — FOOTER: "End Title"

**Scene thesis:** Simple, authoritative end frame. Like rolling credits on a dark field.

**Signature composition:** 3-column footer — Left: TASKAS wordmark + tagline. Center: quick nav links. Right: contact info. All in mist color. Top border: 1px amber gradient (amber→transparent→amber). Copyright below as status-label text.

## Prestige Calibration

1. What does viewer remember after 3 seconds? → The colossal TASKAS wordmark in fog
2. What is intentionally absent? → Hero images, device mockups, card grids, color gradients
3. Which detail makes it expensive? → The amber vertical rule in the About section, used once
4. 30% effect removal test? → Site becomes stronger — the void reads as confidence, not emptiness
5. Interior pages as own scenes? → Each section is a distinct chamber; not copies of the hero
6. Director in grayscale? → Yes — composition and negative space alone carry the Villeneuve grammar
7. Card grid test? → Essential ideas break: the services row expansion, the stat quadrants, the aperture CTA are all non-grid signature moves
8. Standalone poster? → Yes — each section reads as its own poster before shared system is added
